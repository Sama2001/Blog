ESNCM

By Sama | Nov, 2024

Figure1:ESNCM FEB 2020

In February 2020, I had the privilege of performing with the Edward Said National Conservatory of Music (ESNCM) orchestra, a highlight of my musical journey. We played a series of classical pieces, including the enchanting Waltz of the Flowers, across two major venues: Birzeit University and An-Najah University. Standing on stage with fellow musicians, surrounded by the reverberating notes of Tchaikovsky, was both thrilling and humbling. Each performance was filled with a unique energy, as the audience's reactions fueled our passion and made every rehearsal worth it. This experience deepened my connection to music and taught me invaluable lessons in teamwork, discipline, and the power of live performance, moments I’ll cherish forever.

"Music is the divine way to tell beautiful, poetic things to the heart." — Pablo Casals, a renowned Catalan cellist. This quote resonates deeply with my experience performing with the Edward Said National Conservatory of Music (ESNCM) orchestra. It perfectly encapsulates the power of music to communicate beyond words, and my time with the orchestra in February 2020 was a testament to this sentiment.
